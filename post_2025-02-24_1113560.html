Should a novice Android developer give up?

菜鸟 Android 开发该放弃了吗？

I am an individual developer with a technical stack of Java, Kotlin, and Compose. I did full-time development in 2021, then changed careers. I have continued to follow Android development. My Kotlin and Compose related libraries on GitHub have over a thousand stars. I think I have a decent grasp of the related technologies. Last year, I got burned after changing careers and was almost ruined, so I'm preparing to come back to development. I looked at Boss Zhipin for a week and only applied for junior positions in Guangdong province, about 30 resumes including some outsourcers who actively asked for my resume. I didn't expect to get a single interview. Is it because the career change/long unemployment period is a big taboo, or is the internet client really that dead? Should I continue looking for Android work, or change careers? For example, to frontend React/NextJS? I'm a bit more familiar with these two, but I don't have corporate project experience, so I really don't know how to find a job. And after tools like v0 and Cursor mature, will frontend be even more difficult than Android? Please give me some advice!
