Recently, there have been many posts about betrothal gifts. As someone who has been through it, I would like to provide a reference for everyone (a negative example). Let's talk about my situation first: 1 My wife is from a mountainous area in a famous betrothal gift province. Her parents are migrant workers, and there are 3 daughters in the family. 2 My family is in Hebei, my parents are employed in public institutions, and I am an only child. 3 My wife and I married for love. My wife has a doctorate. Her tuition during college was all student loans, which she paid off slowly after graduation. 4 My family bought a wedding house in Beijing and added my wife's name to it. 5 I resolutely refused the exorbitant betrothal gift (I had no money to give the sky-high betrothal gift after buying the house), and my wife also insisted on it. Finally, her parents compromised (but still asked for about 100,000 yuan in gift money under other names). 6 The dowry was 0 yuan. Don't even think about the woman's family helping to decorate the house or buy a car. We didn't get a penny. Don't think it's over. I used to think that I was marrying my wife and didn't care about her family. However, I was too naive. There were a bunch of unhappy things waiting: 1 Don't think that you bought a house in Beijing and added the woman's name, and that your economic level is crushing them. Being nice to their daughter means you will win respect and become a son-in-law they respect? As far as the father-in-law and mother-in-law are concerned, if you don't give a betrothal gift, you owe them money. They will never be interested in you and will never regard you as family. 2 When the wife's family (father-in-law, mother-in-law, sister-in-law) comes to Beijing, they never pay a penny themselves, and try to take as much as they can from us. Round-trip travel expenses, food, drink, and everything else are all paid for by us at their initiative, and they try to spend as much as possible. They don't feel bad spending our money, and even feel a sense of pleasure, feeling spending is earning. They would rather take a plane than a high-speed train, and even take the initiative to ask to buy first-class seats. Let me put it this way, I have never seen their family's payment code. They never pay when I am around. 3 They always come empty-handed to their daughter's house and leave with a big bag of things. My wife's younger sister takes whatever she likes. 4 During festivals, we have to give gifts to the wife's family, but they never give red envelopes to their granddaughter. Over the years, the grandparents have spent less than a few hundred yuan on the child. 5 When we returned to my wife's hometown, a large group of "relatives" came. My mother-in-law said that according to their customs, everyone (not only children, but also uncles and aunts in their fifties and sixties) had to give red envelopes, and we had to have a banquet to entertain them (we naturally covered all the expenses). 6 My mother-in-law hinted that the money we usually give them is too little... Things like this are not outrageous, but there is also almost nothing happy. I have a good relationship with my wife, so I chose to get married. When I look back on it late at night, it is really tiring, helpless, and stupid to get along with such a family. Forever giving and giving, but there is no response, like a bottomless pit. The purpose of posting is to tell those who are not yet married, and those who are hesitating, especially the brothers who have been posting recently, to consider carefully whether you can accept these things. I didn't expect it at the time.
