The website is www.wordflow.click Some say that to learn phonetics, you must pronounce words out loud, and to learn words, you must understand sentences. This is similar to the way machines learn languages. Machines don't look up dictionaries or memorize words. To make a machine understand the meaning of a word, you just need to let it see how the word is used in a sentence. The famous masked language model (MLM) is to mask the word and let the machine look at the context to infer the word meaning. However, most English learning software used by people today, although providing example sentences, still relies on repeatedly looking at words to memorize them. So I made a memory software completely based on sentences. ## 1. Understand English sentences with Cutter A problem I encountered when I was in school was that I memorized English words many times and learned grammar many times, but I still couldn't understand long and difficult sentences. Later, I started practicing learning English through sentences. At that time, I started using a method to break down sentences, decomposing complex long sentences into the main body and modifying components to clarify the sentence structure. The general rule is to identify three long structures: clauses, phrases, and non-predicate verbs, then label the parts of speech to find adjectives and adverbs, and finally label parallel structures. For specific methods, there is a former New Oriental teacher on Bilibili who gives a good explanation ([https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1TY4y1W7aZ/](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1TY4y1W7aZ/)), you can check it out. Practice has proven it to be effective, but the process of breaking down sentences takes a lot of time. Moreover, in the learning process, it is still quite difficult for beginners to accurately identify various clauses, phrases, and non-predicate verbs. So, I made a tool that can automatically identify the clauses, phrases, and non-predicate verbs in a sentence and label the parts of speech. This can save a lot of time and allow for a more accurate understanding of sentence structure. ![Demo gif1]( https://i.imgur.com/5mrhfqx.gif) ## 2.
